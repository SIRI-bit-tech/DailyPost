Accessibility Statement

Our commitment to providing an inclusive and accessible experience for all users

Our Commitment to Accessibility

At The Daily Record Post, we are committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to achieve this.

We strive to ensure that our website meets Level AA compliance with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and user-friendly for everyone.

The guidelines have three levels of accessibility (A, AA, and AAA). We've chosen Level AA as our target for The Daily Record Post website.

Features of Our Accessible Website

Keyboard Navigation

Our website is fully navigable using a keyboard for users who cannot use a mouse

Resizable Text

Text can be resized up to 200% without loss of content or functionality

Screen Reader Compatibility

Content is structured for effective navigation with screen readers

Color Contrast

Sufficient color contrast between text and backgrounds

Compliance Status

We review our website regularly to ensure its accessibility. However, if you experience any problems or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us using one of the methods below.

Compatibility with Browsers and Assistive Technologies

The Daily Record Post website is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers (including NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, and TalkBack)
  • Speech recognition software
  • Screen magnification software
  • Alternative keyboard and mouse input devices

Our website is also designed to be compatible with the current versions of the following browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Accessibility Features in Our Content

Alternative Text

We provide alternative text for all non-decorative images on our website. This allows users with visual impairments to understand the content and context of images using screen readers or other assistive technologies.

Video and Audio Content

We strive to provide transcripts, captions, or audio descriptions for multimedia content to ensure it's accessible to users with hearing or visual impairments.

Data Tables

Tables used on our website include appropriate headers and row/column information to help screen reader users navigate and understand the content effectively.

PDFs and Other Documents

We aim to provide documents in accessible formats. When we create new PDFs or other documents, we work to ensure they are as accessible as possible. If you need any document in an alternative format, please contact us, and we will do our best to assist you.

Limitations & Alternatives

Despite our best efforts to ensure the accessibility of The Daily Record Post, there may be some limitations. Below is a description of known limitations, and potential solutions:

  • User-Generated Content: Comments and user-generated content may not always meet accessibility standards. We encourage our community to consider accessibility when contributing content.
  • Third-Party Content: Some content provided by third parties, such as embedded videos or social media posts, may not be fully accessible. We try to provide alternatives where possible.
  • Older Content: Some older content published before our current accessibility standards were implemented may not be fully accessible. We're working to update these materials over time.

If you encounter an accessibility barrier on our website, please let us know. We're actively working to improve the accessibility of our content and appreciate your feedback.

These technologies are relied upon for conformance with the accessibility standards used.
